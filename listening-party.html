Listening Party

Listening Party (LP) is an app concept for a socially-charged music sharing platform.

The challenge

Working in a team of four (shout out to Syd Chan, Anisa Bashiri, and Christina Cho), we had to create a music-based native mobile app from concept to prototype in ten weeks. The music app industry is broad, ranging from ticketing and event platforms to music-based dating apps, and of course, music streaming services. Through research, we uncovered a void in the crowded market that was validated by our interviews with users. We thought about different ways to full fill these unmet needs, which eventually became the foundation for Listening Party.

Not another streaming app

Clearly, the music streaming industry is saturated. We knew we weren't going to create the next Spotify or Apple Music, but we saw the opportunity in the ever growing popularity of streaming services. Through our research, we learned about where, when, and how people listen to music in social settings.

So what does it do?

We used a feature value matrix to identify the features and settings that mattered most to our users. The scores for Jack, our primary user, were weighted more heavily than our secondary user, Lilian. These personas inspired Listening Party's two main functions: Jukebox mode, where anybody in the Party Room can participate by adding songs to the queue; and DJ mode, where the creator of the Party Room takes the reigns of music selection, but everybody tuned into to the Party Room can listen along.

User flow

We mapped the user flows of our two personas in order to understand how our users would interact with our app. Jack, an active listener, would be more likely to start his own Party Room and invite friends. On the other hand, Lilian, who is a passive listener, would be more likely to join an existing room created by a friend.
